I am an MPS Candidate in Applied Economics and Management at **Cornell University**, and an incoming PhD Student in Economics at **Georgia Tech** (Fall 2026). I hold a B.A. in Economics from the University of California, Irvine.

My research interests lie in **international trade**, **environmental economics**, and **urban and regional economics**, broadly within applied microeconomics. I am particularly interested in how external shocks—trade policy, climate, and environmental regulation—reshape spatial and industrial outcomes in China and other developing economies. My recent work draws on quasi-experimental methods (difference-in-differences, event study, propensity score matching, instrumental variables) and large administrative and transaction-level datasets.

I currently work as a Research Assistant with **Prof. Xuezheng Qin** at Peking University.
