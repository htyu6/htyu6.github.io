<summary><b>Abstract</b> (click to expand)</summary>
<br>
This paper constructs a province–industry panel (16 provinces, 25 industries, 2016–2022) using Chinese Statistical Yearbooks and OECD TiVA data to study the heterogeneous impact of U.S.–China trade tensions on Chinese regional economies. Using a difference-in-differences design with province×industry and year fixed effects, I find that higher GVC exposure leads to larger profit declines, with coastal provinces 3.8 times more affected than inland regions. The technology-intensity gradient follows a U-shape: mid-tech industries suffered the largest losses, while high-tech sectors gained.
