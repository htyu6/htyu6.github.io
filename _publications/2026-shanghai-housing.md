<summary><b>Abstract</b> (click to expand)</summary>
<br>
Drawing on 221,308 residential transactions in Shanghai (2012–2021) and a continuous-treatment difference-in-differences design with community and year-quarter fixed effects, this paper studies how extreme heat exposure during the listing period affects the south-facing premium in residential real estate. Within a projection-bias framework, extreme heat erodes the south-facing premium by approximately 17% at mean exposure levels, with effects concentrated in buildings without elevators and units listed during summer months—consistent with a primacy-effect interpretation of projection bias.
