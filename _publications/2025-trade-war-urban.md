<summary><b>Abstract</b> (click to expand)</summary>
<br>
Using a propensity-score-matched difference-in-differences design across 276 Chinese cities (2010–2023), this paper documents a "passive de-servicification" effect of U.S.–China trade tensions. Cities more exposed to trade barriers experienced a 1.7-percentage-point decline in tertiary sector share, driven primarily by FDI withdrawal. Results are robust to event-study, dose-response, log-level share-illusion checks, wild cluster bootstrap, and randomization inference.
