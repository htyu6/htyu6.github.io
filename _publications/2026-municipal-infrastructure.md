<summary><b>Project Summary</b> (click to expand)</summary>
<br>
This project examines whether differential improvements in municipal wastewater treatment infrastructure during China's 11th Five-Year Plan (2006–2010) reshaped urban industrial composition. Using a continuous difference-in-differences design with city-specific pre-2006 treatment rates as the treatment intensity, and city terrain ruggedness as an instrumental variable, the study tests whether faster-upgrading cities attracted more high-technology firms and fewer pollution-intensive entrants. The proposed analysis combines Five-Year Plan policy targets, firm registry data, and city-level environmental enforcement records, and extends the infrastructure-and-firms literature from transport networks to environmental utilities.
